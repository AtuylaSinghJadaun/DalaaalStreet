==============================================================================
                          DALAAALSTREET
        A Live, Multiplayer Stock-Trading Competition for Events
==============================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. WHAT THIS APP IS (THE BIG PICTURE)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

DalaaalStreet is a real-time, multiplayer stock-market simulation built to be
run as a live event game (college fests, corporate offsites, finance club
competitions, workshops, etc.). One person acts as the "Mentor / Admin" and
controls the flow of the game from a single control panel. Everyone else joins
in TEAMS using a simple numeric code on their own phones or laptops.

The game recreates the lifecycle of a stock market in a controlled, gamified
way:

  - Companies are "listed" with an IPO (initial) price.
  - Teams are given a starting virtual cash balance (e.g. ₹10,00,000).
  - Teams buy shares during an IPO phase.
  - The market then moves through several "rounds". In each round the Admin
    has pre-set how each stock's price changes (driven by a news/event
    headline, e.g. "RBI hikes rates — banking stocks fall").
  - Between price changes, teams trade shares with EACH OTHER (peer-to-peer)
    to maximise their net worth.
  - At the end, all shares are sold off ("liquidated") at final prices and a
    leaderboard ranks every team by net worth.
  - As a bonus finale, an optional live AUCTION lets teams spend their final
    cash on fun reward items (e.g. "Mentor Dinner"), shown on a big screen.

Everything updates LIVE across all devices the instant something happens — a
trade, a price change, a new bid — using a real-time database. No refreshing,
no manual syncing.

The result is a fast, competitive, spectator-friendly trading game that an
Admin can fully configure (companies, prices, rounds, teams, rewards) without
touching any code, all through a guided setup wizard.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. THE TWO TYPES OF USERS
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

A) THE ADMIN / MENTOR (one person, one device)
   - Logs in with a private admin passcode.
   - Sets up the entire game with a 6-step wizard.
   - Controls when the game moves from one phase to the next.
   - Can edit any company, price, round, or team at any time.
   - Runs the optional auction at the end.

B) THE PARTICIPANTS (the teams, many devices)
   - Each team logs in with a 6-digit Team Code given to them by the Admin.
   - They buy shares, trade with other teams, and (optionally) bid in the
     auction.
   - They never see admin controls — only their own team's view.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. THE COMPLETE GAME FLOW (STEP BY STEP, IN ORDER)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The game always moves forward through a fixed sequence of "phases". The Admin
is the only one who advances phases. The sequence is:

   SETUP  ->  WAITING FOR IPO  ->  IPO  ->  ROUND 1 ... ROUND N
          ->  ENDED (Leaderboard)  ->  AUCTION

Here is exactly what happens in each phase.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.1  PHASE: SETUP  (Admin only — building the game)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

When the game has never been configured (or after a full wipe), the Admin
dashboard automatically shows a 6-STEP SETUP WIZARD. The Admin completes each
step in order:

  STEP 1 — TEAMS
     - Choose how many teams will play (1 to 200).
     - Set the starting cash balance every team receives (e.g. ₹10,00,000).
     - The app auto-creates each team with a UNIQUE 6-DIGIT LOGIN CODE and a
       default name ("Team 1", "Team 2", ...). These codes are what the Admin
       hands out to participants.

  STEP 2 — COMPANIES
     - Type in the names of the companies that will be tradable.
     - Add or remove as many as needed.

  STEP 3 — IPO PRICES
     - Set the starting (IPO) price for each company. This is the price teams
       pay per share during the IPO phase.
     - Set the "Minimum IPO Spend" guideline (how much each team is expected
       to invest during the IPO).

  STEP 4 — ROUNDS
     - Define each trading round. For every round you enter:
         * a News/Event headline shown to participants
           (e.g. "Tech boom — IT stocks surge"),
         * the new mean (average) price of EACH company in that round.
     - You can add as many rounds as you want (the app handles any number).

  STEP 5 — END PRICES (THE "ENDGAME")
     - Set the FINAL settlement price for each company. This is the price at
       which every team's remaining shares are sold off when the game ends.
     - These are hidden from participants until the game is over.

  STEP 6 — LAUNCH
     - Review a summary (teams, companies, rounds, balances) and launch.
     - Launching moves the game into "Waiting for IPO".

The Admin can ALWAYS go back later and edit anything (see Section 4).

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.2  PHASE: WAITING FOR IPO  (The lobby)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

   - Participants can now log in with their Team Code.
   - They land on a "Waiting" screen ("Waiting for IPO Release...").
   - No trading happens yet — this is the holding lobby while everyone joins.
   - The Admin presses "Release IPO" when ready to begin.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.3  PHASE: IPO  (Teams buy their first shares)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

   - Each team sees every company as a card showing its IPO price, the number
     of available shares, and a small price chart.
   - A team enters a quantity and presses BUY. The cost (quantity x IPO price)
     is deducted from their cash balance, and the shares are added to their
     portfolio (their "holdings").
   - Buying more of the same company correctly averages the purchase price.
   - The app prevents buying more than a team can afford.
   - Every team's cash balance updates live as they spend.

NOTE ON PARTICIPATION: When a team logs in during the IPO window, it is marked
as a participant. After the game moves past the IPO, teams that never joined
the IPO are blocked from entering — this stops late-comers from joining once
trading has begun. (Teammates on extra devices can still join as long as their
team participated.)

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.4  PHASE: ROUND 1, ROUND 2, ... ROUND N  (The trading floor)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

This is the heart of the game. The Admin advances the market one round at a
time ("Start Round 1", "Start Round 2", ...). In each round:

  THE MARKET MOVES
     - Each company's price changes to the mean price the Admin set for that
       round. A news headline for the round is displayed to explain why.
     - A live PRICE CHART for each company shows how its price has moved from
       the IPO through every round so far. Hovering a point on the chart shows
       which round it was and the exact price (e.g. "Round 2: ₹105.40"). The
       line is green if the price is up versus IPO, red if down.

  TEAMS TRADE WITH EACH OTHER (PEER-TO-PEER)
     - A team creates a trade request: choose Buy or Sell, a company, a
       quantity, a price, and the OTHER TEAM they want to trade with.
     - IMPORTANT RULE — THE PRICE BAND: any offered price must be within
       +/- 20% of the current round's mean price for that company. (If the
       mean is ₹100, valid offers are ₹80 to ₹120.) This keeps trading
       realistic and prevents absurd prices.
     - The request is sent to the target team, who sees it as an INCOMING
       request and can ACCEPT or REJECT it.
     - On ACCEPT, the system automatically: moves the shares from seller to
       buyer, moves the cash from buyer to seller, and updates both teams'
       portfolios. It also checks the buyer has enough cash and the seller
       has enough shares (rejecting the trade if not).
     - Requests expire automatically after 3 minutes if not answered.

  EACH TEAM SEES, LIVE:
     - Their net worth (cash + value of all holdings at current prices),
     - Their cash balance,
     - Their portfolio with profit/loss per holding,
     - Incoming and outgoing trade requests (with counts).

The Admin keeps advancing rounds until the final round is done.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.5  PHASE: ENDED  (Liquidation + Final Leaderboard)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

When the Admin presses "End Game":

  - LIQUIDATION: every team's remaining shares are automatically sold at the
    FINAL (Endgame) prices the Admin set in Setup Step 5. The proceeds are
    added to each team's cash. After this, a team's cash balance IS its final
    net worth.
  - Teams are taken to the FINAL RESULTS screen, which shows:
       * Their final rank (#1, #2, ...),
       * Their final net worth and total profit/loss versus their starting
         balance,
       * A FINAL PRICE-HISTORY GRAPH for every company (IPO -> each round ->
         Endgame), with hover tooltips showing the round and exact price,
       * The full LEADERBOARD ranking every team by net worth, with medals
         for the top three and their own team highlighted.

  IMPORTANT: Every active team appears on the leaderboard with a rank — even a
  team that sold off ALL of its shares and is holding only cash. No
  participating team is ever dropped from the final standings.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.6  PHASE: AUCTION  (Optional grand finale)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

After the game, teams can spend their final cash on fun reward items in a live
auction run by the Admin.

  - The Admin creates auction items (name, description, starting bid) in the
    Auction Management screen, then presses "Start" on one item.
  - Starting an item opens a BIG-SCREEN "STAGE" view (meant to be projected),
    showing the item, the current highest bid, the leading team, and a
    countdown.
  - On their own devices, teams place bids: quick "+₹50,000" / "+₹1,00,000"
    buttons, or a custom amount. Each bid must beat the current highest and
    must be within the team's cash balance.
  - A short countdown resets every time a new bid comes in ("Going once...").
  - The Admin presses "End & Sell". The winning team's cash is deducted and
    the item is added to their inventory, where they can mark it as "used"
    when they redeem it.
  - Only one auction item can be live at a time. The Admin can run as many
    items in sequence as they like.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. ADMIN POWERS (BEYOND SETUP)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Admin has a full control panel ("Command Center") and a "Properties" area:

  GAME CONTROLS (Command Center)
     - A single row of phase buttons (Release IPO, Start Round 1, Start
       Round 2, ..., End Game, Auction). Only the NEXT valid action is
       highlighted and clickable; past phases are locked. This makes it
       impossible to skip or break the sequence.
     - The round buttons are generated automatically from however many rounds
       were configured — add 4 rounds and you get 4 round buttons.
     - Live stats: active teams, companies listed, number of rounds.

  PROPERTIES (edit anything, anytime)
     - COMPANIES: edit a company's name, industry, IPO price, shares, etc.
     - ROUNDS: a clean list that always shows IPO first, every trading round
       in the middle, and Endgame last. You can edit each round's headline and
       its per-company prices, edit the IPO prices, edit the Endgame (final)
       prices, and add new rounds (which slot in neatly between IPO and
       Endgame).
     - TEAMS: edit a team's name or cash balance, regenerate its login code.

  RESET (start over safely)
     - SOFT RESET: clears all trades, holdings, bids and winnings, and resets
       every team's cash back to the starting balance — but KEEPS your
       companies, rounds, prices, and teams. Perfect for re-running the same
       configured game with a fresh group.
     - HARD RESET: wipes everything and returns to the Setup Wizard for a
       completely new game.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. WHAT MAKES IT WORK WELL (HIGHLIGHTS)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  - FULLY LIVE: every device updates instantly — trades, prices, bids,
    balances. Spectators can watch the leaderboard and auction in real time.
  - NO-CODE SETUP: the Admin configures the whole game (any number of
    companies, rounds, teams, and reward items) through a guided wizard.
  - SELF-CONTAINED & FAIR: the price band, fund/shares checks, phase locking,
    and participation rules keep the game orderly without policing.
  - MOBILE-FRIENDLY: participants play on their phones with a clean, modern,
    dark "trading arena" interface.
  - SPECTATOR MODE: a dedicated big-screen auction stage makes the finale a
    live event.

6. TEST GUIDE — HOW TO TEST THE APP
==============================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.1  DEVICE REQUIREMENT (IMPORTANT)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

A MINIMUM OF 3 DEVICES (or 3 separate browser windows/profiles) IS REQUIRED to
properly test this app, because the core gameplay is multiplayer and
peer-to-peer:

   - 1 device = THE ADMIN (runs the control panel and advances phases).
   - 2 devices = TWO PARTICIPANT TEAMS (needed because trading happens BETWEEN
     two teams — one sends a trade request, the other accepts/rejects it).

You cannot fully test trading or the auction with fewer than 3 devices. (Tip:
if you don't have 3 physical devices, use three different browsers or
incognito/private windows so each has its own separate login session.)

For a richer test, use 4+ devices: 1 admin + 3 or more teams, plus optionally
one extra screen opened to the big-screen Auction "Stage" view.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.2  FULL TEST WALKTHROUGH
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

DEVICE A = ADMIN.  DEVICES B and C = TEAMS.

  1) SET UP THE GAME (Device A — Admin)
     - Open the app, go to the Admin login, and enter the admin passcode.
     - If prompted with the Setup Wizard, complete all 6 steps:
         Step 1: create at least 2 teams, set a starting balance.
         Step 2: add at least 2 companies.
         Step 3: set an IPO price for each company.
         Step 4: create at least 2 rounds with prices and headlines.
         Step 5: set Endgame (final) prices for each company.
         Step 6: Launch.
     - Write down (or note) two of the generated 6-digit Team Codes.

  2) TEAMS JOIN (Devices B and C)
     - On each device, open the app and log in using a different Team Code.
     - You should land on the "Waiting for IPO" screen.

  3) RUN THE IPO (Device A presses "Release IPO")
     - On Devices B and C, the IPO screen appears.
     - On each team, buy some shares of one or more companies. Confirm the
       cash balance drops and the purchase succeeds. Try to overspend and
       confirm it is blocked.

  4) RUN A TRADING ROUND (Device A presses "Start Round 1")
     - Devices B and C move to the Trading Floor.
     - Confirm the Market Watch shows the round's prices and the price charts
       appear. Hover a chart point and confirm it shows the round + price.
     - On Device B, create a trade request: choose Buy/Sell, a company, a
       quantity, a price WITHIN +/-20% of the shown mean price, and select
       Team C as the target. Send it. (Test the band by trying a price outside
       +/-20% and confirming it is rejected.)
     - On Device C, find the INCOMING request and ACCEPT it.
     - Confirm both teams' cash and portfolios update live and correctly.
     - (Optional) Advance to Round 2 and repeat; confirm prices and charts
       update.

  5) END THE GAME (Device A presses "End Game")
     - Devices B and C show the Final Results screen.
     - Confirm every team appears on the leaderboard with a rank (including a
       team that sold all its shares), the price-history graphs end at the
       "Endgame" point, and profit/loss looks correct.

  6) RUN THE AUCTION (Device A switches to the Auction phase)
     - In Auction Management, create an item and press "Start" (a big-screen
       Stage window opens — optionally view it on a 4th screen).
     - On Devices B and C, place competing bids. Confirm bids must beat the
       current highest and cannot exceed a team's balance, and the highest
       bidder updates live.
     - Press "End & Sell". Confirm the winner's cash is deducted and the item
       appears in their inventory.

  7) RESET (Device A)
     - Try a SOFT RESET and confirm teams/companies/rounds remain but progress
       is cleared.
     - (Optional) Try a HARD RESET and confirm it returns to the Setup Wizard.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.3  WHAT TO VERIFY (CHECKLIST)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  [ ] Team codes log in; wrong codes are rejected.
  [ ] Phases only advance via the Admin, in the correct order.
  [ ] IPO purchases deduct cash and create holdings; overspend is blocked.
  [ ] Round prices and live charts update; hover tooltip shows round + price.
  [ ] Trade price must be within +/-20% of the round mean price.
  [ ] A trade between two teams moves both shares AND cash correctly.
  [ ] End Game liquidates holdings at Endgame prices.
  [ ] Leaderboard shows EVERY team with a rank (even a fully-cashed-out team).
  [ ] Auction: bids must beat the current high and fit the team's balance;
      winner is charged and receives the item.
  [ ] Soft reset keeps config; hard reset returns to setup.
  [ ] All of the above reflect LIVE across every device without refreshing.
